Battle of Stalingrad (1942-1943)

A turning point on the Eastern Front, where the Soviet Union decisively defeated the German army.

D-Day, Battle of Normandy (1944)

The largest seaborne invasion in history, leading to the liberation of Western Europe from Nazi occupation.

Battle of Midway (1942)

A pivotal naval battle in the Pacific Theater, where the United States Navy significantly weakened the Japanese navy.

Battle of Britain (1940)

The first major campaign fought entirely by air forces, and a critical victory for the United Kingdom against Germany.

Battle of El Alamein (1942)

Marked the turning point in the North African Campaign, with the Allies defeating the Axis forces.

Battle of Kursk (1943)

One of the largest tank battles in history and a significant Soviet victory against German forces.

Battle of Berlin (1945)

The final major offensive of the European theatre, leading to the fall of Berlin and the end of Nazi Germany.

Pearl Harbor (1941)

The surprise military strike by the Japanese against the United States, leading to the U.S. entering World War II.

Battle of the Bulge (1944-1945)

The last major German offensive on the Western Front, which ultimately failed, hastening the end of the war.

Operation Barbarossa (1941)

The German invasion of the Soviet Union, which marked a significant escalation of the war and ultimately led to the downfall of the Third Reich.
