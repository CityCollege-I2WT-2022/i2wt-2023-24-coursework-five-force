Introduction

World War II, a global conflagration that lasted from 1939 to 1945, was a defining moment in history that saw nations around the world becoming embroiled in a conflict of unprecedented scale and devastation. While the war was initially met with hesitancy and a desire for neutrality among the American populace, a series of events eventually propelled the United States into active participation. This essay aims to explore the multifaceted journey that led to American involvement in World War II, examining the complex interplay of political, economic, and social factors that shaped the nation's path from isolationism to intervention.
