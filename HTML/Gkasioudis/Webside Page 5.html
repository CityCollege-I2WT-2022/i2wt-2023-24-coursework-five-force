The watershed moment that definitively drew the United States into World War II was the surprise attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941. The Japanese assault on the Pacific Fleet stationed in Hawaii resulted in significant destruction and loss of life, shaking the foundations of American security. President Roosevelt's famous address to Congress the following day, declaring December 7th as "a date which will live in infamy," marked the formal entry of the United States into the global conflict. The unanimous declaration of war against Japan, quickly followed by Germany and Italy, united the American populace in a common cause against the Axis powers.
